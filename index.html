Dynamics

What is Dynamics?

Dynamics is the branch of mechanics that studies the motion of bodies under the influence of forces. It covers kinematics, describing motion (position, velocity, acceleration), and kinetics, analyzing forces causing motion, including rotation and vibration. Key concepts include momentum, energy, and torque, applied to systems like vehicles, machinery, and projectiles. Dynamics is crucial for engineering designs, ensuring safe and efficient motion in structures and devices.

1. Kinematics (Rectilinear Motion)

Position: \( s = s_0 + v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \) (constant acceleration)

Velocity: \( v = v_0 + a t \)

Acceleration: \( a = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d^2 s}{dt^2} \)

Velocity-Position: \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2 a (s - s_0) \)

Average Velocity: \( v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{s_{\text{final}} - s_{\text{initial}}}{t} \)

2. Kinematics (Curvilinear Motion)

Position Vector: \( \vec{r} = x \hat{i} + y \hat{j} + z \hat{k} \)

Velocity: \( \vec{v} = \frac{d\vec{r}}{dt} \), magnitude \( v = |\vec{v}| \)

Acceleration: \( \vec{a} = \frac{d\vec{v}}{dt} = \frac{d^2 \vec{r}}{dt^2} \)

Tangential/Normal Components:

Tangential: \( a_t = \frac{dv}{dt} \)

Normal: \( a_n = \frac{v^2}{\rho} \) (\( \rho \) = radius of curvature)

Projectile Motion (no air resistance):

Horizontal: \( x = v_0 \cos\theta \cdot t \)

Vertical: \( y = v_0 \sin\theta \cdot t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2 \)

Range: \( R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\theta)}{g} \)

3. Kinetics (Newton’s Laws)

Newton’s Second Law: \( \sum \vec{F} = m \vec{a} \)

Weight: \( W = m g \)

Friction:

Static: \( F_f \leq \mu_s N \)

Kinetic: \( F_f = \mu_k N \)

4. Work and Energy

Work: \( W = \int \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = F s \cos\theta \) (constant force)

Kinetic Energy: \( T = \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \)

Potential Energy:

Gravitational: \( V_g = m g h \)

Elastic: \( V_e = \frac{1}{2} k x^2 \)

Work-Energy Principle: \( T_1 + V_1 + W_{\text{non-conservative}} = T_2 + V_2 \)

Conservation of Energy: \( T_1 + V_1 = T_2 + V_2 \)

Power: \( P = \frac{dW}{dt} = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} \)

5. Impulse and Momentum

Linear Momentum: \( \vec{p} = m \vec{v} \)

Impulse: \( \vec{J} = \int \vec{F} \, dt = \Delta \vec{p} \)

Conservation of Momentum: If \( \sum \vec{F}_{\text{ext}} = 0 \), then \( \vec{p}_1 = \vec{p}_2 \)

Coefficient of Restitution: \( e = \frac{v_{2,\text{relative}} - v_{1,\text{relative}}}{u_{1,\text{relative}} - u_{2,\text{relative}}} \)

6. Rotational Kinematics

Angular Position: \( \theta \)

Angular Velocity: \( \omega = \frac{d\theta}{dt} \)

Angular Acceleration: \( \alpha = \frac{d\omega}{dt} = \frac{d^2 \theta}{dt^2} \)

Constant Angular Acceleration:

\( \omega = \omega_0 + \alpha t \)

\( \theta = \theta_0 + \omega_0 t + \frac{1}{2} \alpha t^2 \)

\( \omega^2 = \omega_0^2 + 2 \alpha (\theta - \theta_0) \)

Linear-Angular Relations: \( v = \omega r \), \( a_t = \alpha r \), \( a_n = \omega^2 r \)

7. Rotational Kinetics

Moment of Force (Torque): \( \vec{\tau} = \vec{r} \times \vec{F} \), magnitude \( \tau = F r \sin\theta \)

Newton’s Second Law for Rotation: \( \sum \vec{\tau} = I \vec{\alpha} \)

Moment of Inertia: \( I = \int r^2 \, dm \)

Common shapes:

Thin rod (about center): \( I = \frac{1}{12} m L^2 \)

Disk/Cylinder: \( I = \frac{1}{2} m R^2 \)

Angular Momentum: \( \vec{L} = I \vec{\omega} \)

Conservation of Angular Momentum: If \( \sum \vec{\tau}_{\text{ext}} = 0 \), then \( \vec{L}_1 = \vec{L}_2 \)

8. Rolling Motion

Condition for Rolling: \( v_{\text{cm}} = \omega R \)

Kinetic Energy: \( T = \frac{1}{2} m v_{\text{cm}}^2 + \frac{1}{2} I \omega^2 \)

Acceleration (incline): \( a = \frac{g \sin\theta}{1 + \frac{I}{m R^2}} \)

9. Vibrations

Simple Harmonic Motion:

Displacement: \( x = A \cos(\omega t + \phi) \)

Velocity: \( v = -A \omega \sin(\omega t + \phi) \)

Acceleration: \( a = -A \omega^2 \cos(\omega t + \phi) \)

Angular Frequency: \( \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \) (mass spring), \( \omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{L}} \) (pendulum)

Period: \( T = \frac{2\pi}{\omega} \)
